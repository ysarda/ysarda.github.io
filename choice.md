I don’t like that I have to choose.

I couldn’t tell you if it’s indecision or something else; something more intrinsic, less scorned by society. The multi-disciplinary environments of our childhoods are gone, replaced with a money gulching machine that expects results but accepts adults. Imperfect, and honed to an edge, taught to love and work and do, but don’t deviate just rEmeMBeR tO vOtE!

I don’t like that I have to choose.

I don’t like that success in this day and age still means that I can calculate, but slower than a calculator, and compute, but slower than a computer. That an archaic system still governs our education with no thought of the integration of actual progress, not just words in a history book. One billion people are suddenly connected, but, no, you’re right, I should put my phone up in class. It’s not like the next generation of adults will be completely connected to one another. It’s not like the development of technology is exponentially increasing, that it’s getting harder and harder to predict future trends based on past data, that almost everything I can do right now is more easily done by a machine. You’re right. I’ll put my phone up.

I don’t like that I have to choose.

Having to choose people is the worst, I’ve learned. Trust me: at 20 and a ½ years old, I’m an expert. Having to live with their choices, to know that they are entitled to everything and nothing, that they need no reason. Anyone can bugger off for any reason, and it’s fine. It’s fine. I’m not heartbroken. It’s fine. How do you plan, and anticipate, and predict, when the ones you love deserve the world, even from you, especially from you, taken from you. And we’re told to love one yet sneered at when we break apart, when the idea of gem-studded shackles go from romantic to kinda terrible, when we pour our heart and soul and mind into someone, never thinking to save enough for, us.

The system is broken.

I don’t like that I have to choose.
